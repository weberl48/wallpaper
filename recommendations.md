# Wallpaper Recommendations

**Room:** Dark green board & batten wainscoting, walnut floors, blacked-out ceiling, bronze sconces, antique statement pieces

---

## Tier 1: Best Matches

| Vibe | Pick | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Antiques are the star** | Bronze-Green Metallic Grasscloth or Charcoal Sisal | Devon Grace's rule: texture when art/sconces go in front. The bronze-green metallic literally matches your sconces. Designer's choice. |
| **Wallpaper is the star** | Antonina Vella Speakeasy in Black/Gold | Metallic arches glow under bronze sconces (warm-metal amplification). Proven in real speakeasy basement projects. ~$56/roll. |
| **Best of both worlds** | York Bird & Blossom Chinoiserie in Black | Metallic finish catches sconce light like grasscloth, but the pattern is a statement. Works above wainscoting as a "cropped scene." Timeless with antiques. |

## Tier 2: Strong Contenders

| Vibe | Pick | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Maximum personality** | House of Hackney Empire Midnight | Drinking animals in bowler hats on midnight blue. Premium at ~$298/roll but unforgettable. "Collected curiosity" vibe matches antique statement pieces. |
| **Timeless classic** | William Morris in a dark colorway | Golden Lily Midnight or Strawberry Thief. Emily Henderson's "restraint" approach. Subtle enough to let antiques shine, iconic enough to be interesting. Won't date. |
| **Moody dramatic** | Dark Moody Florals on black | Black background ties into ceiling, florals soften the room. Studio McGee's contrast principle: light floral elements provide visual relief in a "full moody" room. |

## Tier 3: Specific Vibe Picks

| Vibe | Pick | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **English pub / library** | Earl Dark Green Tartan | Plaid + dark green B&B + walnut + leather chairs = instant private club. Works perfectly with older antiques and bronze. |
| **Prohibition speakeasy** | Speakeasy Black + Dark Tropical Leaves | Mix Art Deco arches on main walls with a tropical accent. Real speakeasy projects layer multiple textures rather than going uniform. |
| **Eclectic collector** | Stalking Tiger Black Moss or Dark Botanical Animals | Bold enough to be a conversation piece, dark enough to stay cohesive. Your antiques become part of the story. |

## Key Design Principles

- **Dark background wallpapers will feel most cohesive** -- you're "full moody" with a black ceiling and dark green walls, so don't fight it with light wallpaper
- **Metallic elements in wallpaper amplify bronze sconces** -- gold, copper, and bronze tones in the paper will catch and reflect warm light
- **Texture over pattern if art/sconces are the focal point** -- grasscloth lets your collected pieces speak; pattern makes the walls the show
- **Medium-scale patterns work best above wainscoting** -- except murals and chinoiserie, which look great "cropped"
- **Mix materials for authenticity** -- the best speakeasy projects avoid "themed" and instead layer different textures, ages, and finishes
- **Order samples** -- see how patterns look under your actual bronze sconce lighting at different times of day
